Watch where each map glows: in the empty edges where new species hide, or in the crowded centre. That single choice explains the whole ranking on slide 11.

Why the ranking: whoever labels the empty spaces wins

Illustrative, not BCI data. Each strategy is the real algorithm run on one synthetic 200-point cloud where common species form dense clusters and rare species sit in the sparse gaps and edges. The map shown is the exact quantity each selector maximises. The mechanism it exposes — the sparser the ground a method labels, the more rare species it finds (rank correlation ≈ 0.9 here) — is why CoreSet, novel_margin and MaxHerding win and TypiClust loses; the measured numbers on real crowns (8 seeds, DINOv3, multiple-testing corrected) are on slide 11.
